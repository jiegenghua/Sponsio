# Brand & trademark policy

The Sponsio source code is licensed under [Apache 2.0](LICENSE). The
Apache 2.0 license covers the *code*. It does **not** transfer
trademark or brand rights.

This document spells out what the Sponsio name and marks cover, what
you can do without asking us, and what requires permission.

---

## Marks

The following are trademarks (registered or pending) of Sponsio Labs:

- **Sponsio**™ — the product name
- **Sponsio Cloud**™ — the managed offering
- The Sponsio wordmark and logo (in [`assets/`](assets/))

Apache 2.0 grants the right to use, modify, and redistribute the code.
It does *not* grant rights to these marks.

---

## You can do this without asking

- **Use Sponsio in your own project, internal tools, products, and
  services.** Apache 2.0 covers all of this.
- **Refer to "Sponsio" in factual statements**, e.g. "powered by
  Sponsio", "compatible with Sponsio contracts", "Sponsio-style
  enforcement", in blog posts, talks, papers, comparison tables, or
  documentation.
- **Distribute unmodified Sponsio binaries / source.**
- **Submit patches and contributions** under the contributor terms
  in [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md).

---

## You need permission for this

If you want to do any of the following, please [open an issue](https://github.com/SponsioLabs/Sponsio/issues/new)
or email [hello@sponsio.dev](mailto:hello@sponsio.dev) first:

- **Distribute a modified version of Sponsio under the name
  "Sponsio"** or any name that includes "Sponsio". Apache 2.0
  permits forks, but forks must use a different name and
  not represent themselves as the canonical Sponsio project.
  *Examples requiring rename:* `MySponsio`, `Sponsio-Plus`,
  `Sponsio-Cloud-Free`, `OpenSponsio`.
- **Operate a hosted Sponsio service under the Sponsio name**
  (paid or free). Running a managed `sponsio.example.com` competing
  with Sponsio Cloud is a trademark use, not a code use.
- **Use the Sponsio logo or wordmark in your product UI, package
  listing, or marketing.** Linking to the Sponsio website or repo
  with our logo is fine; using it as part of *your* product's brand
  is not.
- **Imply endorsement, partnership, or certification by
  Sponsio Labs.** Examples: "Sponsio Certified", "Official Sponsio
  Plugin", "Sponsio Partner".
- **Sell merch, courses, training, or certification using the
  Sponsio name.**

## Forks

Apache 2.0 explicitly permits forks. Sponsio Labs welcomes them.

When you fork Sponsio, please:

1. **Rename.** Use a name that doesn't include "Sponsio" so users
   know they're getting your project, not ours.
2. **Update the README** to make the fork relationship clear,
   ideally with a one-line "Forked from
   [SponsioLabs/Sponsio](https://github.com/SponsioLabs/Sponsio)".
3. **Don't reuse our logo or wordmark** as your project's logo.

That's it. We don't gate the code behind a CLA-with-relicensing
clause; the code stays Apache 2.0 forever (see
[`docs/oss_scope.md`](docs/oss_scope.md)).

---

## Why this exists

Sponsio Labs operates a commercial product (Sponsio Cloud) on top of
this OSS engine. A clear brand boundary protects users from confusion
("which Sponsio is the real one?") and keeps the OSS project's
reputation in our hands. It also lets us publish new versions, security
advisories, and CVE responses under a name users can trust to be
authoritative.

If your use case isn't clearly covered here, ask. We'd rather
authorise something that's fine than have you guess.
